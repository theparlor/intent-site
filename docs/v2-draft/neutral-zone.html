▸ Adoption · Phase 2: The Neutral Zone

Weeks 3–8 are the hardest part

You've stopped the old way but you're not fluent in the new way yet. Bridges calls this the Neutral Zone — the period of "chaos, creativity, and confusion" where most transformations quietly die. Intent owes you a playbook for this period, not just a new beginning. Here it is.

▸ First, explicit permission

Week 3 of Intent adoption: your team is bad at sprints (you stopped) and bad at Intent (you haven't internalized it yet). That is normal and temporary. Say it out loud in a team meeting. Write it on the board. Name the chaos, because hiding it accumulates shame.

The seven scaffolds

Each scaffold is a structural element to put in place during the neutral zone. They are not permanent — most dissolve into the new rhythm by week 8 or 9. But they hold the team together while the new muscle memory develops.

01

Permission to be bad at both

Name it explicitly at the start of week 3. Write a team agreement: "We will be bad at the old way (we stopped) and bad at the new way (we're learning). For the next 4 weeks, that is expected. We will not evaluate individual performance against either standard during this period."

/team-meeting Week 3: read this aloud, sign the agreement, post it visibly
02

A weekly ritual that replaces the retro

Not the retro itself — that belongs to the old way. Something new and structured: a 30-minute weekly "transition check." Questions: What felt worse than last week? What felt better? What do we miss? What do we want to try differently next week? The check is explicitly about the transition, not about work output.

/ritual Weekly 30-min Transition Check, same day same time, last thing Friday
03

A safety valve back to the old way

Teams need to know they can go back if Intent doesn't work. Irreversible adoption creates panic. Reversible adoption creates curiosity. Explicit agreement: "If, after 8 weeks, we conclude that Intent is worse for us than what we had, we go back. No blame. Intent is not a religion." Without this valve, people spend the neutral zone worried about being trapped, which prevents learning.

/commit Week 9 checkpoint: continue / adjust / revert. Decision owned by team, not by manager.
04

A visible practice board

Not Intent's event stream — that's work output. The practice board is a meta-view: "what are we trying this week about HOW we work?" Small experiments like "we're trying to capture signals within 5 minutes of noticing friction" or "we're trying to shape one spec per day using the four-persona protocol." The board surfaces the adoption experiments and makes them discussable.

/board Transition Experiments — weekly experiments, outcomes, learnings
05

Weekly check-in on psychological safety, explicit

Not a vibes check. A structured conversation. Questions: Does capturing a signal feel safe? Does disagreeing with a spec feel safe? Has anyone felt judged by a trust score this week? Has anyone worried their signals will be read against them? The check-in is structured to catch psych safety degradation early, when it's still recoverable. See the Safety Contract.

/check-in Psychological Safety: 15 min, last Wednesday of each week in the zone
06

Ending dates on old tools

Bridges is explicit: gradual fades don't work, they just extend the zone. Commit a specific date when standups stop, when the sprint board is archived, when story points are retired. Publish the dates. Honor them. The team needs a clear ending, not a lingering twilight.

/schedule "We stop doing standups on 2026-05-01" — post, don't slip
07

Transition buddies (not mentors)

Pair each team member with a buddy for the transition. The buddy is not a mentor — they're someone to vent to about the transition without it being a team discussion. Quick 1:1s once a week. The buddy pattern distributes the emotional labor of the neutral zone across pairs instead of concentrating it in the team lead.

/pair Assign buddy pairs Week 2 (before ending), reassess Week 6

What changes at week 8

Around week 8, the scaffolds start to feel unnecessary. Signal capture becomes reflexive. The spec-shaping protocol feels like normal work. The weekly transition check feels forced. That's the sign the neutral zone is ending. At that point:

  • Retire the weekly transition check (replace with standard observation rituals)
  • Reduce the practice board (experiments become fewer, more targeted)
  • Keep the psychological safety check — this one is forever, not just the neutral zone
  • Dissolve the transition buddies (keep them as optional peer support)
  • Schedule the Week 9 checkpoint decision: continue / adjust / revert

▸ If the zone stays chaos past week 8

Don't push through. If week 8 still feels like chaos, something is wrong — probably the infrastructure prerequisites (no rollback, no automated testing, no visible measurement) or the cultural prerequisites (psych safety too low, or Theory X dynamics surfacing). Read "when NOT to adopt Intent" and be honest about whether you're in that category. Rolling back is a legitimate outcome, not a failure.
